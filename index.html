Badminton is a racquet sport played using racquets to hit a shuttlecock across a net. Badminton is a fast-paced racket sport played by hitting a shuttlecock over a net using lightweight rackets. It can be played in singles (one vs one) or doubles (two vs two), and it’s popular both casually and competitively around the world.

A short history of badminton.

Badminton has surprisingly old roots. Games similar to it were played thousands of years ago in places like ancient Greece, China, and India, where people used simple paddles to keep a feathered object in the air.

The modern version of badminton developed in India during the 1800s. A game called “Poona” was played by British army officers stationed there. When they returned to England, they introduced the game to high society. The sport got its current name from Badminton House, a large estate in England owned by the Duke of Beaufort. In 1873, a game of Poona was played there at a party, and people began referring to the sport as “the badminton game.”

By The late 1800s:

  • The first official rules were written in England
  • The Badminton Association of England was formed in 1893
  • The first major competition, the All England Open Badminton Championships, started in 1899

Badminton continued to grow internationally. In 1934, the Badminton World Federation (originally called the International Badminton Federation) was established to govern the sport globally. Badminton became an official Olympic sport at the 1992 Summer Olympics, which helped boost its popularity even more.

Today

Today, badminton is especially popular in countries like China, Indonesia, India, Denmark, and Malaysia. It’s known for being one of the fastest racket sports in the world, with shuttlecock speeds exceeding 300 km/h in professional matches.
